Punnett Square Question Generator.

Objective: Randomly generate questions for students to practice predicting outcomes for simple trait Mendalian genetics.


Manually creating and grading questions for student practice requires significant time. Moreover, at any given time, students are at different levels of understanding and individually require different amounts of practice. The question generator allows student to practice - as much as needed - until they can consistently solve a series of questions.

Background


What are Punnett Squares?


In genetics, a Punnett Square is an organizational approach to predicting trait inheritance from parent to offspring. For most of the genes in our genome, we have two copies for any one gene; each of these copies is called an allele. You can think of alleles as being variants of a gene that can confer a particular trait.

Our two alleles can either be the same variant, or they can be different variants, but it’s important understand that these variants are for the same trait. Alleles can either be dominant or recessive, which determines which expression of the trait will be observed. For simple traits – or traits that are determined by a single gene – we can use Punnett Squares to determine the probability of an offspring inheriting a particular trait.

During reproduction, just one of allele from each parent is passed down to an offspring. The Punnett Square demonstrates four possible outcomes of allele inheritance. Then, based on the possible allele compositions, we can infer probabilities for genetic makeup and trait expression.
